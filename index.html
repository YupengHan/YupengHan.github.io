Research Interests

  • My research interest lies in Computer Vision and Robotic Perception. More specifically, I work on leveraging the depth information and GPU parallel computation power to solve vision problems like outdoor scene object detection for autonomous driving, and indoor object poses estimation for robot grasping.

  • In addition to using deep networks to extract feature representations, my research focuses more on exploiting GPU parallelization's advantage to solve vision problems. We propose a method to reconstruct multiple objects in different poses and then select the pose that most similar to the observed data as the prediction result. We test our work on the YCB dataset and achieve better accuracy than the SOTA data-driven approaches.

  • Though the outdoor scene is more extensive and can no longer be presented as a dense format like depth image, which makes the research more challenging, I still conducted satisfying results on outdoor object detection, which is going to submit to ICRA 2021.
